Introduction

The PlayStation 2 (PS2) is a home video game console developed by Sony Computer Entertainment.

Released during the year 2000, it became one of the best-selling consoles of all time.

It was a revolutionary console for its time.

Hardware

The PS2 featured a 128-bit Emotion Engine CPU.

It also included DVD playback capabilities, which made it a popular choice for home entertainment.

Backward compatibility with PlayStation 1 games was a key feature of the PS2.

  • The PlayStation 2's main central processing unit (CPU) is the 64-bit R5900-based "Emotion Engine", custom-designed by Sony and Toshiba.
  • GH-001 Motherboard - Length : 302mm (11.9 inches) Width : 79mm (3.1 inches) TDP : 79W
  • 128-bit graphics
  • 4MB eDRAM
  • 4MB VRAM
  • 8MB memory card
  • MIPS 34MHz second CPU for PS1 game emulation

Games

The PS2 had a massive library of games, including iconic titles like Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas.

Other popular games included Final Fantasy X and Metal Gear Solid 3.

Its diverse game library appealed to a wide range of players.

  • Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas (Best selling game of the PS2)
  • Final Fantasy X
  • Metal Gear Solid 3
  • Kingdom Hearts
  • God of War
  • Gran Turismo 4

Legacy

The PS2's success paved the way for future PlayStation consoles, such as the PlayStation 3.

It remains a beloved system among gamers and collectors.

Its legacy continues to influence modern gaming, including the PlayStation 5.

  • Best-selling console
  • Influenced future consoles
  • Beloved by collectors
  • Iconic game library

Fun Facts

The PS2 sold over 155 million units worldwide, making it the best-selling console of all time (second are the Nintendo DS with 156.1 million units sold).

It was the first console to support DVDs, which contributed to its popularity.

Production of the PS2 continued until 2013, 13 years after its release.

Despite the fact the PS2’s been discontinued since 2013, no other gaming console has managed to top it—in fact, the next closest actively-sold consoles, the PS4 and Nintendo Switch, are each more than 40 million units behind.

More than 4000 games has been developped for the PS2.
